## The Challenge

Recotap is an account-based marketing platform built for B2B teams. When I joined, the product had grown organically — features were added as customer requests came in, resulting in an interface that felt stitched together rather than designed.

The core problem: **users couldn't connect the dots between their data and their actions**. The platform had powerful capabilities buried under layers of complexity.

## What I Did

I started by spending two weeks talking to users — SDRs, marketing managers, and ops leads who used Recotap daily. The patterns were clear: people understood ABM conceptually but struggled with the product's mental model.

- Mapped the entire user journey from account identification to campaign execution
- Identified 14 friction points across the core workflow
- Proposed a simplified information architecture that grouped features by intent rather than function
- Built and tested prototypes with 8 existing customers

## The Design System

Alongside the product redesign, I built Recotap's first design system from scratch. Not a Figma library with pretty components — a working system with clear rules for spacing, color usage, component behavior, and content patterns.

The system now serves three product teams and has reduced design-to-dev handoff time significantly.

## Outcome

The redesigned platform saw a measurable improvement in user activation and feature discovery. More importantly, the team now has a shared language for design decisions — we argue about the right things instead of debating button colors.

> Good tools should feel like they already know what you want to do next.
